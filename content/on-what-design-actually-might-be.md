Design is what happens when a human being refuses to accept the world as a fixed condition.
It is not the "act" of empathy, not entirely, and it is not art, not entirely.
It is something closer to a posture; one that holds both of those things and more, without collapsing into either. It is the practice of noticing: what is broken, what is missing, what is present but misaligned with the people it is supposed to serve. And then the practice of making - not as a separate act, but as the same act, continued.

The person who sees what needs to be made and the person who makes it should be, ideally, the same person in the same moment. This isn't a rejection of collaboration. It's a recognition that the act of seeing is already a form of making - and that when we sever the two, we often lose the most honest part of both. Every observation in itself is a draft.

We are moving, however, into something post-phenomenological. Experience itself is increasingly designed at the infrastructure level, by systems and agents that have no model of what experience actually is - that cannot be surprised, cannot be hurt, cannot want. This is not a reason to panic. It is a reason to take your own capacity for noticing extremely seriously. That capacity is not a soft skill. It may be the last irreducibly human one.

The world has always been diminished by people who were too narrow to see sideways.

Design is sideways.
