# On Subtlety in Design

In an era of hyper-stimulation, designing for the web has often become a shouting match. Who can build the wildest animations? Who can create the most intrusive modals? I've come to believe that the very best design, especially for a reading experience, is the design you don't notice.

## Glassmorphism, Brutalism, and Beyond

We've seen trends come and go. But long-form writing requires something more timeless. It requires restraint. The margins need to be wide enough to let the text breathe, and the line height tall enough to guide the eye comfortably from one sentence to the next.

Consider the layout of traditional print media, like the editorial pieces in high-end magazines. The white space is intentional. It carries just as much weight as the ink. 

### Implementation

When building this space, I wanted to use tools like `GSAP` to add a bit of life. But how do you use powerful animation libraries without making it feel like a circus? 

You stick to the basics:
* **Opacities:** Fading elements in rather than sliding them across the screen.
* **Micro-interactions:** A hover state that is just a touch slower than expected.
* **Delays:** Making the user wait just a fraction of a second reveals intention.

> The interface should feel alive, but never impatient.

I plan to explore these concepts more in future essays. For now, enjoy the quiet.
